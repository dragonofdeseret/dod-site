Biography

Christopher Shenefelt is a writer and artist from Northern Utah interested in comparative theology and phenomenology. His work examines consciousness, perception, and the symbolic structures through which experience is organized and understood.

Drawing from Mormonism, Daoism, Zen, and related traditions, his writing moves between metaphysical inquiry and direct observation. Shaped by lifelong encounters with the limits of embodied experience—chronic pain, clinical insomnia, sustained meditation, and extensive engagement with lysergic acid diethylamide and dimethyltryptamine—his work approaches consciousness where identity loosens, perception turns back on itself, and the field of view expands into our shared headless condition.

His background spans professional theatre management and technical work performed for various Fortune 100 companies. Though his central concern remains the careful study of the phenomenology of life.

He tries to live by Stephen Sondheim’s artistic dictum:

Content dictates form.

Less is more.

God is in the details.

All in the service of clarity.
