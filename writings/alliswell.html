All is Well

May 11, 2018

LSD was against everything I had ever been taught. Not only were there the physiological dangers, there were the potential for flashbacks, and the risk of losing control of your mind altogether; taking such a substance could leave any of us vulnerable to spiritual deception, trickery—demons. Additionally, any knowledge or wisdom one believes they may have gained while in a state of psychedelia pales in comparison to any of the revelations which God could pass through to them naturally. Countless tales of bad trips full of fear, anguish, and delusion, people jumping out of windows and getting seriously hurt or dying, the possibility of the trip never ending. The major crux for a young Mormon though, is that it is against the Word of Wisdom. 

However, at the age of twenty-eight, having spent my entire life living under the alleged wisdom of those words, I found that I was living a life full of fear, anguish, and delusion anyway. In addition to these odd, altered states of conscious circumstances of my earlier childhood, around the age of fourteen I was struck with widespread, inexplicable chronic pain. So at this time, I considered: if I’m not in control of many of the faculties which cause me distress...

By that point, over the past fifteen years, I had tried at least two dozen different prescribed medications...

Not to mention, during this point in my life, I had recently become disaffected with the church—existential crisis mode had certainly reached an apex.

So in mid-May of 2018 I took it. I had my first tab of LSD, and there I sat, waiting in my armchair for psychedelic apocrypha to take its eternal grip and thrust me into the abyss.

It would not take hold nearly in the ways I had been taught to expect...

This night was an overall peaceful and joyous experience filled with incredible bouts of new, welcome, and wondrous confusion...

A few hours in, after waking from a nap, I was lead outside and into the darkness of a Latter-day Saint church parking lot.

On the way there I was shown a tree.

It was breathing so gently yet with such strength...

Standing directly at its trunk base, slowly looking directly upward, I saw the acutely detailed structures of the bark...

The church street and park lamps were not illuminated that night...

I looked up, and I witnessed the stars in the midst of a rapid, diamond-laden cosmic warfare...

I had to look away, since I had been struck slightly off balance, and overcome with tears and emotion at the beauty...

As I looked back up, the helicoid of the universe began to decelerate its rapid racing pace...

As they made their approach, stopping to float just above me, they came together as one to form a magnificent cloud...

As they did so, the wind chimes modulated, and that erupting tune of the glockenspiel stars I had heard earlier arose again as heaven’s harmony began singing:

“Tis better far for us to strive
Our useless cares from us to drive
Do this, and joy your hearts will swell,
All is well! All is well!”

They surrounded me, these individualized, bubbling stars of high luminosity...

It was then and there that I truly realized: these are useless cares.

I knew that all of my worries—in the grand scheme of things—are useless cares serving me no purpose in moving forward through life.

I would come to find that this is the same message found in many Eastern philosophies: suffering is caused by desire.

Personally, as a then fresh ExMormon, I had such an aching desire to know...

Only now, in the middle of a Latter-day parking lot, in the middle of the night, and unbeknownst to me, in the middle of the n-Lyrids meteor shower...

Furthermore, I recognized that this very striving to drive them away was in and of itself a useless care as well...

This embrace from the stars was a spiritual liberation from gravitational and atmospheric pressures...

I would come to find that I felt and feel as Terence McKenna once expressed:

“I am the most optimistic person you have ever met. I’m absolutely convinced that there is nothing wrong...

I had reached an end of my useless cares, and so, thanks to a host of angels sent from the peaceful end of the heavens to embrace me...

This is how I know.
